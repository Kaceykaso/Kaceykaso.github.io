Personal Statment

u-ni-corn [yoo-ni-kawrn] n. 1. A person who is adept at both visual design of UI and UX elements, as well as writing production code; to be both right- and left-brained.

I am a unicorn. I began my college career at The Art Institute of CA-Orange County, majoring in Animation, right after high school. I had been reading, and drawing my own, comic books my entire life, and had decided I wanted to do nothing but that as a career. My parents only had high school diplomas, and being the oldest of four kids, I was the first to try this thing called “college”. I had no idea what a “for-profit university” was, or what “accredited” meant; I just knew the spokeswoman for the college was nice, and the school had “Art” right in the name. I was meant to go there!

Fast forward two years, of a three year program, and I have decided I want to change majors. People do that all the time, right? No reason why I cannot change it, except for the fact that the major I want is not one of the four offered. I needed to go to a “real college”. I withdraw from The Art Institute, and it is not until I try to enroll in a university that I find out that none of my credits will transfer. Not one. I had to start all over at a junior college, as if I had not just spent 2 years in school. Starting over was discouraging, but at least at a junior college I could take night classes, something AI did not have. I have always had to work a full time job while going to school, but I have always made progress regardless. Despite taking on a full load of 16 units, I was still model employee at Albertsons, being promoted time and again, and becoming the youngest Albertsons store manager in southern California.

It took me twice as long to get my AA from Miramar College and transfer; and it has taken me twice as long to graduate from UCSD, all while working full time. I have had jobs in Geek Squad, IT infrastructure and applications, and software engineering; and I have usually been the only woman. In each new position, I have had to fight, prove my worth and knowledge, and prove that I do not sleep around, more than any of my male counterparts. Rumors would usually fly regardless, but again, I was always a model employee and promoted. Currently, I am working in my field, working a second part time job, all while finishing the last two classes for my bachelors degree. This June I will be the first in my family to graduate college.

I have learned, and began to love, programming throughout my career; snowballing from frontend web design, to backend database programming. This all led to other, far geekier interests outside of work, like building computers, robots, and gaming. I am equally comfortable discussing Constructivist or Bauhaus philosophy, as I am comparing and contrasting MySQL to Oracle SQL, or SVN to Git. I can relate how PHP being rendered on the server effects Javascript and CSS being rendered on the client side, and how cross-site scripting attacks screw all of it up. I can translate design speak into code, bridging the gap between UI Designers and Software Engineers.

I am a unicorn, because I can take an idea from sketches, to wireframes and rapid prototypes, to full-stack production code. I am unicorn, because this is very rare, and highly desired. And it is because of this particular set of skills, that I have acquired over a long career, that I will change the world. Because being a woman in tech has made me work harder and faster than my coworkers, and made my resolve that much more absolute. Because I have talked in front of incoming freshmen, as a representative for my major, and heard their fears and concerns. Because every chance I get I give back, as the Professional Development Chair for Women in Computing at UCSD, I help others in my shoes and give the advice I wish I had recieved a decade earlier. Because I do not want the next generation of women in computing to have to suffer the rumors and hardships I did. I want to be successful in my job, doing what I love, free of prejudice, while helping other women in my field do the same. I am a unicorn because I can, and will, do all of these things. And more.
