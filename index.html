This site is meant to help give explanation, examples, and analysis on user interface design, specifically for software and limited hardware. Here you will find web and logo design case studies, electronics projects and their struggles in documentation, and various arguments and writings by the author, Kacey Coughlin.

The Design section is dedicated to graphic and digital design examples; pieces intended to only be in digital form, either for software or the web. This is typically categorized by a lower resolution image with a stricter color palette.

The Print section is where you will find design work intended for print, or non-digital means. This usually means a higher resolution image, and richer color scheme.

The Web section is just as it sounds: examples of work done for the web, either in websites or full web applications. These range from simple front-end only work, such as HTML, CSS, and Javascript, to more complex work involving MySQL or Oracle databases.

The Misc section is anything that does not directly fit into one of the above categories, such has robotic or hackathon projects.

The About page gives a more detailed biography of the author, as well as her design principles, and more resources.
